Analysis of San Francisco Salaries (2011-2014)¶

Introduction

In this Python script, I am taking a deep dive into a dataset that reveals the salaries of San Francisco city employees from 2011 to 2014. The dataset, named 'SFSalaries2011_2014.csv,' gets loaded into a Pandas DataFrame, essentially a digital table that makes it easy for us to work with the data. This DataFrame becomes our home base for exploring various aspects of the dataset using numbers, charts, and targeted questions.

Once the dataset is loaded, my exploration kicks off with a focus on basic numerical insights. I start by examining straightforward statistics like average pay and the distribution of salaries, aiming to grasp the fundamental characteristics of the compensation landscape. This numerical analysis provides us with a quantitative lens to identify trends, variations, and significant observations within the dataset.

This script not only uncovers the San Francisco city employee salaries over the four-year period but also serves as a demonstration of the capabilities of Python, Pandas, and related tools in the realm of data exploration. Through a blend of simple language, numerical analyses, and compelling visuals, my script try to show the narrative work into the salary data, contributing to a comprehensive understanding during this specific time frame.
